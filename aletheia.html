Alpha

Film-Negative Transform

Alpha defines the working space. It moves images into and out of film-negative domains, enabling printer-light style balancing and exposure-driven shot control.

Printer Lights

RGB exposure controls that behave like traditional lab timing. Adjustments interact naturally with contrast and saturation.

Negative Density

Controls that respect the nonlinear response of film emulsion. Push and pull behave as expected.

Transform Modes

Multiple film-negative encodings available. Match the working space to the source or creative intent.

Bidirectional

Enter and exit negative space freely. Use as input transform, output transform, or creative tool.

Omega

Look Development System

Omega defines the look. It reconstructs film behavior through parametric models and spectral simulation, driven by a piecewise 1D contrast curve. Grain, halation, and Chen & OKLAB color models are integrated, with unified SDR and HDR output.

Film Contrast Curves

Piecewise 1D curves that model real film response. Toe, shoulder, and midtone behavior—adjustable independently.

Grain Synthesis

Physically-based grain that responds to exposure and color. Not a texture overlay—grain that lives in the image.

Halation Model

Light scatter through emulsion layers. Blooming highlights that wrap color naturally, not digitally.

Unified Output

Single toolset for SDR and HDR delivery. The look translates across formats without rebuilding.

Phi

Shot-level balance

Phi is a shot-level solution designed for colorists who want speed without compromise.

It consolidates exposure, contrast shaping, color separation, density, and perceptual balance into a single, coherent toolset. Instead of stacking nodes and fighting interactions, Phi lets you shape an image holistically—one pass, one intent, one result.

Exposure & Density

Scene-referred exposure control with density adjustments that preserve highlight and shadow detail.

Contrast Shaping

Pivot-based contrast with separate highlight and shadow controls. No clipping, no crushing.

OKLAB Saturation

Perceptually uniform saturation and vibrance. Colors shift in hue less as saturation increases.

Tetrahedral Interpolation

Secondary color adjustments that maintain smooth gradients and avoid color banding.

Exposure

Scene-referred exposure in stops. Adjusts the image before contrast is applied, preserving the full dynamic range of your source.

Contrast & Pivot

Pivot sets where contrast hinges. Raise it to protect highlights, lower it to protect shadows. Contrast expands or compresses around that point.

Density

Controls how much light passes through the image. Increase for richer blacks, decrease for a lifted, faded look. Works independently of exposure.

Saturation & Vibrance

OKLAB-based saturation for perceptually uniform adjustments. Vibrance targets less-saturated areas, leaving already-vivid colors alone.

Secondary Colors

Tetrahedral interpolation for targeting specific hues. Adjust individual color ranges without artifacts or banding at the boundaries.

Helix

Texture & surface character

Helix is a look-development solution focused on texture, structure, and surface character.

It is designed to refine how an image feels. Helix shapes micro-contrast and perceived detail in a controlled manner. The result is depth and tactility that reads naturally across formats and delivery specs.

Frequency Separation

Isolate and adjust detail at different spatial scales. Skin texture, fabric weave, architectural detail—each controllable independently.

Micro-Contrast

Local contrast adjustments that add depth without haloing. Edges stay clean, surfaces gain dimension.

Surface Character

Shape how materials render. Matte vs. glossy, rough vs. smooth—texture defines the look as much as color.

Detail Redistribution

Move detail between frequency bands. Soften skin while retaining hair detail. Sharpen eyes without sharpening pores.

Frequency Bands

Separate your image into low, mid, and high frequency layers. Each band captures detail at a different spatial scale—from broad tonal shapes to fine texture.

Detail Amount

Control how much detail exists in each band. Reduce high-frequency detail to soften skin, boost mid-frequency to add depth to fabric and architecture.

Local Contrast

Add dimensionality without affecting global contrast. Surfaces gain depth and presence while the overall image balance remains unchanged.

Texture Response

Shape how textures render across the image. Control whether surfaces read as matte or glossy, rough or smooth—independent of color.

Edge Handling

Adjustments stay clean at boundaries. No haloing around high-contrast edges, no ringing artifacts. Detail changes blend naturally.
